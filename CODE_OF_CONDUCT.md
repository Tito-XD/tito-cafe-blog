# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want Revamped Astro Zozo to be a respectful, welcoming project for everyone who opens an issue, proposes a change, or joins a discussion.

As contributors and maintainers, we pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity or expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that helps create a positive environment include:

- showing empathy and patience
- giving constructive feedback
- respecting different viewpoints and lived experiences
- focusing critique on ideas, code, and design decisions rather than people
- being considerate in public and private communication

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, threats, or intimidation
- insulting, demeaning, or discriminatory language
- personal attacks or bad-faith hostility
- publishing someone else's private information without permission
- any conduct that would reasonably make others feel unsafe or unwelcome

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, branch-based collaboration, preview and deployment feedback, and other public or private communication directly related to this repository.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

Repeated or severe violations may result in temporary or permanent restrictions from participation in the project.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please contact the maintainer at:

- `maintainer@example.com`

All reports will be reviewed and handled as carefully and respectfully as possible.
